Global Distribution & Wealth Correlation

Michelin-starred restaurants cluster in high-income regions, revealing a strong connection between economic prosperity and culinary excellence. These visualizations show how geography and wealth shape the landscape of fine dining.

1

Global Distribution Map

Interactive world map showing the geographic concentration of Michelin-starred restaurants across continents.

Geographic Concentration: Western Europe and East Asia contain the vast majority of Michelin-starred restaurants. France, Japan, Italy, and Germany lead globally, with dense clusters in Paris, Tokyo, and Northern Italy. The United States shows concentration primarily in major metropolitan areas.

2

Stars by Income Group

Normalized distribution showing how Michelin stars concentrate in high-income economies.

Economic Correlation: High-income countries account for over 90% of all Michelin stars across all rating levels. This stark concentration demonstrates that fine dining excellence clusters where disposable income, tourism infrastructure, and culinary investment converge.

3

Regional Distribution

Absolute counts of star ratings across World Bank geographical regions.

Regional Patterns: Europe & Central Asia leads with over 5,000 starred restaurants, followed by East Asia & Pacific with approximately 3,000. North America, despite economic strength, shows significantly fewer establishments, highlighting Europe's centuries-old culinary traditions.

Cuisine, Pricing & Star Ratings

From traditional cuisine styles to pricing patterns, discover what defines Michelin-starred dining and how star ratings correlate with cost and culinary traditions.

Global Star Distribution

Total count breakdown of one, two, and three-star restaurants worldwide.

Key Insight: One-star restaurants represent approximately 85% of all Michelin-starred establishments, demonstrating the exclusivity of higher ratings. The scarcity of three-star restaurants (less than 2%) reflects the exceptional difficulty of achieving culinary perfection.

Culinary Diversity

Global dining trends show modern cuisine leading in popularity, while classic culinary traditions remain influential pillars of Michelin recognition.

6

Most Popular Cuisines

Proportional breakdown of cuisine types among all Michelin-starred establishments.

Key Insight: Modern cuisine dominates with over 6,000 restaurants, reflecting contemporary dining trends. Traditional styles like Japanese, Italian, and French maintain strong presences, showing the enduring value of culinary heritage.
